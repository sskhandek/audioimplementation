How to interpret our visualization

There are three main areas to look at: theme, genre, and correlation

The direction of the bars indicates whether the correlation is negative or positive. If you hover over the bar you're interested it in, it will tell you what correlation it had as well as whether it was negative or positive; additionally the bar you are currently hovering over will turn purple to indicate that it is being further exammined. The color of the bars indicates which genre we're talking about, and the center label tells you what the theme the subgraph refers to (in our particular visualization we chose to look the common themes of: money/fame, women, drugs/alcohol, and family.

Some sample patterns to look out for

Relationships were overwhelmingly viewed as positive

In country music a lof the songs talked about their lady loves while in rap, they talked mostly about their posses, so in both genres women/relationships were viewed as positive in part because it was an indicator of being in love or being successful.

Alcohol in Country Songs vs Drugs in Rap Songs

Drugs were talked about in reference to escapism in rap songs while in country, alcohol was referred to often as a social lubricant, thereby garnering a more positive set of sentiments.

Rap tended to view family more negatively than Country

Rap songs tended to talk a lot about their hometowns, and more often than not, how hard life was there. Also, a common trope is the lacking of a good family presence. In country, family/home were referred to in the context of nostalgia and longing to return to the family.
